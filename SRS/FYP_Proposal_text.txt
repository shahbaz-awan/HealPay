Abstract 

3 

Medical Billing in the U.S. healthcare system is slow, complex, and prone to errors. 

Small clinics and busy practices often rely on manual data entry and coding, which 

leads  to  incorrect  ICD-10/CPT  assignments,  frequent  claim  denials,  and  delayed 

payments. This project develops a web-based automated medical billing system that 

streamlines the entire revenue cycle from patient intake to insurer submission. A key 

feature is an interactive patient intake module where patients can describe their visit 

(text  or  voice);  the  system  uses  this  input  to  auto-populate  demographic  and  visit 

details, reducing initial data entry errors. Doctors then review the record and assign or 

confirm ICD-10 and CPT codes. The platform applies OCR and NLP to extract clinical 

information from notes, maps terms to standard codes, and generates CMS-1500/UB-

04  claim  forms.  Before  submission,  an  automated  validation  engine  checks  claims 

against payer rules and an ML model predicts the likelihood of rejection so staff can 

fix issues proactively. Validated claims are then formatted for electronic submission to 

insurers. So by combining automation, clinician oversight, and predictive analytics, the 

system aims to reduce denials, speed reimbursements, and improve billing transparency 

for providers and patients.

1. Vision Document 

1.1. 

Introduction 

1.1.1.  Problem Statement: 

 Medical  billing  in  the  United  States is highly  complex  and  mostly  manual, 

leading  to  frequent  coding  errors,  missing  patient  details,  and  high  claim 

rejection  rates.  These  problems  cause  delays  in  payments  for  hospitals, 

financial losses for clinics, and confusion for patients. There is a need for an 

automated  system  that  can  capture  patient  information,  assist  doctors  in 

assigning accurate ICD-10/CPT codes, validate claims before submission, and 

improve transparency in the billing process. 

1.1.2.  Objectives 

●  To develop a web-based system that automates the medical billing process. 

●  To capture patient details during intake and reduce manual entry errors. 

●  To assist doctors in assigning accurate ICD-10 and CPT codes. 

●  To  generate  and  validate  claim  forms  before  submission  to  insurance 

companies. 

●  To use machine learning for predicting claim acceptance or rejection. 

●  To  provide  dashboards  for  billing  staff  and  patients  for  better  tracking  and 

transparency. 

1.2. 

Motivation 

The complex nature of medical billing system often overwhelms both healthcare staff 

and patients. The errors in coding and claim submission cause unnecessary delays and 

financial  stress.  Our  motivation  is  to  apply  modern  technologies  and  automation  to 

simplify this process, reduce the burden on clinics, and make billing more transparent 

for patients.

1.3. 

Overview 

Our  proposed  system  is  a  web-based  medical  billing  platform  which  is  designed  to 

handle  the  complete  process  from  the  intake  of  patient  details  to  claim  submission. 

Patients  will  provide  their  details  during  intake,  which  are  recorded  in  the  system. 

Doctors  then  confirm  and  assign  ICD-10  and  CPT  codes,  while  the  system 

automatically  generates  claim  forms  and  validates  them  against  insurance  rules.  A 

machine  learning  module  predicts  the  chances  of  rejection,  allowing  errors  to  be 

corrected before submission. The platform also offers dashboards for staff and patients 

to track claims and view billing details in a clear and structured way. 

1.4. 

Functionality  

The proposed system provides end-to-end automation of the medical billing process, 

covering  patient  intake,  coding,  claim  generation,  validation,  and  submission.  Each 

module is designed to reduce manual effort, improve accuracy, and ensure transparency 

for both healthcare providers and patients. 

The main functionalities of the system are described below: 

1.4.1.   Patient Intake Module 
Patients will provide their personal and medical details through an easy-to-
use interface. This reduces manual errors and ensures that all necessary data 
is captured at the start of the billing process. 

1.4.2.  OCR and NLP Processing 
The system will process uploaded medical notes or reports using OCR and 
NLP to extract key medical information. This feature helps in reducing 
manual effort and improves coding accuracy. 

1.4.3.  Coder’s Coding Support 
Coders can directly assign or validate ICD-10 and CPT codes for each patient 
record. This ensures  that coding  remains accurate and  aligned  with  medical 
standards. 

1.4.4.  Code Recommendation System 
Based on the details entered by the patient, the system will recommend 
suitable ICD-10 and CPT codes. The doctor will then review and confirm or 
modify these recommendations before final submission. 

1.4.5.  Claim Form Generator 
The system will automatically generate claim forms such as CMS-1500 and 

UB-04  with  all  the  required  patient,  provider,  and  coding  information. This 

ensures standardization and saves time for billing staff.

1.4.6.   Validation Engine and Claim Prediction 
Before submission, the system will validate claims against insurance rules and 

guidelines. Any missing or incorrect information will be flagged for correction. 

Then we will use a machine learning model that will analyze claims and predict 

the likelihood of acceptance or rejection. This allows staff to resolve potential 

issues before claims are submitted. 

1.4.7.   Dashboard 
The dashboard will provide billing staff with tools to track submitted, pending, 

and rejected claims. Patients will also have access to their bills in a clear and 

transparent format. 

1.4.8.  Notifications  
The system will send alerts and updates to both staff and patients about claim 

status changes, such as submission, approval, or rejection. 

2. Tools and Technologies 

2.1. 

Backend (Python with Flask)  

Python will be used as the core programming language due to its 

simplicity and rich ecosystem. Flask will serve as the backend  

framework for building REST APIs and managing business logic.  

2.2. 

Frontend(React.js) 

The user interface will be built using React.js to ensure a responsive 

and user-friendly experience. This will allow patients, doctors,  

and billing staff to interact with the system through a web browser. 

2.3. 

Database (PostgreSQL) 
A relational database such as PostgreSQL will store patient records, 

billing data, and claim history. PostgreSQL is preferred for its  

support of structured and semi-structured data formats. 

2.4.
